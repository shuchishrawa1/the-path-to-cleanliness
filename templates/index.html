The Path to Cleanliness

Cleaner, healthier villages around Chandigarh.

We are a student-led NGO working in rural areas near Chandigarh University, including villages like Mamurpur, Gharuan and Morinda, to fix broken waste systems and strengthen rural sanitation.

Why Rural Cleanliness Matters

In many villages around growing cities, waste management has not kept pace with development. Without proper dustbins, collection points or dump yards, households are often forced to dump or burn waste in the open, harming both the environment and public health.

The Waste & Health Challenge

  • Open dumping and burning pollute air, contaminate water sources and degrade soil.
  • Stagnant garbage and blocked drains attract mosquitoes and rodents, increasing diseases like diarrhoea, dengue and malaria.
  • Sanitation workers and villagers handle mixed waste without protection, facing daily exposure to hazardous materials.

What We Saw in Villages

In villages around Chandigarh University, we observed scattered garbage, irregular waste collection and a lack of basic facilities such as dustbins, collection points and proper dump yards.

Many residents rely on burning or open dumping of waste, which directly contributes to polluted surroundings and health risks for the entire community.

Who We Are

The Path to Cleanliness is an emerging non-governmental organisation started by students near Chandigarh University. Our work currently focuses on rural regions around the campus, beginning with villages such as Mamurpur, Gharuan and Morinda.

We align our efforts with the Swachh Bharat Mission and focus on practical, ground-level action: assessing village cleanliness, raising awareness, supporting better waste systems and coordinating with local authorities.

What We Do

Assess Villages

  • Door-to-door surveys to understand household waste practices.
  • Identification of open dumping and burning spots.
  • Mapping of dustbins, drains and waste collection routes.

Engage & Support

  • Meetings with villagers, youth, women’s groups and Panchayat members.
  • Awareness activities on hygiene, waste segregation and safe disposal.
  • Clean-up drives and coordination with Municipal Corporation and local bodies.

Our Impact & Vision

Through continuous field engagement, we aim to improve visible cleanliness in public areas, increase awareness about hygiene and promote better waste handling practices in rural communities.

With adequate support, we plan to expand our activities to more nearby villages, run regular school-based awareness programs, promote waste segregation and composting, and help communities maintain cleanliness independently over time.

Together, we can reduce sanitation-related health issues and build cleaner, safer and more dignified rural living spaces.
